Wearable spectroscopy devices can experience under-saturation or over-saturation issues. Whenever a device is placed in a new setting, such as a different position, ambient lighting condition, or skin tone, even the electronic circuits are not guaranteed to produce consistent illumination or readings. Therefore, an intensity calibration algorithm is needed to ensure that the device can self-modulate its intensity so that it consistently operates within the optimal reading range (60\% $\sim$ 80\% of the maximum readings). You will use a test spectroscopy device, the [Lumos](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3569502) prototype, in this project.
